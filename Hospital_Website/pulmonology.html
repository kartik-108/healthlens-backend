Pulmonology Department Overview

The Pulmonology Department at HealthLens Hospital focuses on diseases and disorders of the lungs and respiratory system, including chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), asthma, pneumonia, tuberculosis, and lung cancer. Our pulmonologists offer advanced diagnostics and treatment plans for acute and chronic respiratory conditions.

We provide a combination of clinical expertise, modern imaging, pulmonary function testing, and interventional procedures to ensure precise diagnosis and effective treatment.

Department Facilities 🏥

  • Advanced pulmonary function labs and spirometry testing.
  • Bronchoscopy and endoscopic lung interventions.
  • High-resolution imaging: CT scan, X-ray, and MRI for respiratory evaluation.
  • Critical care and ventilator support for severe respiratory illnesses.
  • Specialized programs for smoking cessation, asthma, and COPD management.

About Pulmonology Science 🌬️

Pulmonology is the branch of medicine that deals with the respiratory system. Healthy lungs are essential for oxygen exchange, energy production, and overall well-being. Pulmonologists diagnose, treat, and manage respiratory diseases, ensuring proper breathing function and quality of life.

Causes of Respiratory Disorders ⚠️

Respiratory problems may result from infections, allergies, smoking, environmental pollutants, genetic predisposition, or chronic conditions like asthma and COPD. Timely intervention and preventive measures are essential to maintain lung health and prevent complications.

Symptoms and What to Do 🩺

Symptoms include shortness of breath, persistent cough, wheezing, chest pain, fatigue, or recurrent respiratory infections. Patients experiencing these signs should consult a pulmonologist promptly. Pulmonary rehabilitation, medication, and lifestyle modifications form integral parts of treatment.

Treatment 🏥

Treatments vary depending on the condition and may include inhalers, medications, oxygen therapy, pulmonary rehabilitation, lifestyle changes, and minimally invasive procedures such as bronchoscopy. In severe cases, surgical interventions or lung transplantation may be considered.
