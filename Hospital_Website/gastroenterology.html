Specialized care for digestive health — stomach, intestines, liver, pancreas, and more, ensuring comfort and wellness.

Gastroenterology Department Overview

The Gastroenterology Department at HealthLens Hospital is dedicated to the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of digestive system disorders. This includes diseases of the esophagus, stomach, intestines, pancreas, liver, and gallbladder. Our team provides expert care for conditions ranging from acid reflux and ulcers to liver diseases and inflammatory bowel disorders.

Using modern diagnostic technologies and advanced endoscopic procedures, we ensure accurate diagnosis and effective treatment tailored to each patient.

Department Facilities 🏥

  • Advanced endoscopy and colonoscopy units for precise diagnosis.
  • ERCP (Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography) for bile duct and pancreatic conditions.
  • State-of-the-art imaging services: MRI, CT, and ultrasound for abdominal evaluation.
  • Liver function, fibrosis, and viral hepatitis testing labs.
  • Specialized clinics for pediatric gastroenterology and nutrition-related disorders.

About Gastro Science 🍎

Gastroenterology is a branch of medicine focusing on the digestive tract and its associated organs. This system is responsible for digesting food, absorbing nutrients, and eliminating waste. A healthy digestive system ensures proper energy balance and overall well-being. Disruptions can cause discomfort, malnutrition, or chronic illnesses.

Causes of Digestive Disorders ⚠️

Digestive problems may result from poor diet, stress, infections, alcohol use, smoking, genetic factors, or autoimmune conditions. Conditions like GERD, IBS, fatty liver disease, and Crohn’s disease are becoming increasingly common due to lifestyle and dietary changes.

Symptoms and What to Do 🩺

Symptoms of gastroenterological diseases can include persistent abdominal pain, bloating, nausea, vomiting, unexplained weight loss, jaundice, changes in bowel habits, or blood in stool. Patients experiencing such signs should seek medical attention immediately. Early diagnosis and treatment can prevent complications and improve recovery.

Treatment 🏥

Treatment options in gastroenterology depend on the underlying cause. These may include dietary adjustments, medications to control acidity or inflammation, antibiotics for infections, lifestyle changes, or endoscopic and surgical interventions. Regular screenings such as colonoscopies are recommended for early detection of polyps or cancer.
