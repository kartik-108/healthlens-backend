Nephrology Department 💧

Specialized care for kidney diseases, dialysis, and renal health management.

Nephrology Department Overview

The Nephrology Department at HealthLens Hospital focuses on kidney health, prevention, diagnosis, and management of kidney diseases. Our nephrologists provide expert care for conditions such as chronic kidney disease, acute kidney injury, hypertension-related kidney issues, and electrolyte imbalances.

We emphasize early detection, personalized treatment plans, and continuous monitoring to maintain renal function and overall health. Dialysis and transplantation support are available for advanced kidney conditions.

Department Facilities 🏥

  • Comprehensive kidney function tests and diagnostic labs.
  • Hemodialysis and peritoneal dialysis facilities.
  • Renal biopsy and advanced imaging for kidney evaluation.
  • Management of hypertension and electrolyte disorders.
  • Pre- and post-kidney transplant care and monitoring.

About Nephrology Science 💧

Nephrology is the branch of medicine focused on the kidneys, their function, and associated disorders. Nephrologists diagnose and treat acute and chronic kidney diseases, manage dialysis, and monitor systemic conditions affecting renal health. This branch combines clinical expertise with laboratory diagnostics, imaging, and therapeutic interventions.

Causes of Kidney Disorders ⚠️

Kidney diseases can result from diabetes, hypertension, infections, autoimmune disorders, genetic factors, and toxins. Poor hydration, excessive medication, or untreated systemic diseases may also contribute. Early detection and lifestyle management are key to preventing long-term damage.

Symptoms and What to Do 🩺

Symptoms include swelling (edema), changes in urine output, fatigue, nausea, hypertension, and electrolyte imbalance. Patients should undergo regular check-ups, maintain hydration, control blood pressure, and follow dietary guidelines. Timely nephrology consultation is essential for early intervention.

Treatment 🏥

Treatment options include medication for blood pressure, diabetes, and kidney function stabilization, dialysis for advanced kidney failure, and dietary & lifestyle management. Transplantation is an option for end-stage kidney disease. Continuous monitoring and patient education are essential for long-term kidney health.
