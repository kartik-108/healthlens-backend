About Oncology Science 🧬

Oncology is the branch of medicine focused on cancer research, diagnosis, treatment, and prevention. It encompasses medical oncology, surgical oncology, and radiation oncology. Oncologists study the biology of tumors, genetic factors, and environmental causes to design effective treatment strategies. Personalized medicine ensures that therapy is tailored according to the specific type and stage of cancer.

Causes of Cancer ⚠️

Cancer can result from genetic mutations, environmental exposures, lifestyle factors such as smoking or alcohol, infections, or chronic inflammation. Certain cancers are hereditary, while others develop due to accumulated cellular damage over time. Prevention and early detection are crucial to reduce risk and improve outcomes.

Symptoms and What to Do 🩺

Symptoms vary depending on cancer type but may include unexplained weight loss, persistent fatigue, lumps or swelling, abnormal bleeding, or changes in skin/moles. Regular screenings and timely consultation with an oncologist are essential for early detection. Lifestyle modifications, vaccination where applicable, and healthy habits are preventive measures.

Treatment 🏥

Treatment is tailored based on cancer type, stage, and patient condition. Options include surgery, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, immunotherapy, targeted therapy, and supportive care. Multidisciplinary management ensures optimal outcomes and improved quality of life. Patient counseling, nutrition guidance, and rehabilitation are integral to comprehensive care.
